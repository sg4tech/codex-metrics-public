# Human oversight cost may be a larger hidden expense than AI API cost

Single-hypothesis file.

### H-028 — Human oversight cost may be a larger hidden expense than AI API cost

- Status: `active`
- Created: `2026-04-04`
- Statement:
  - The current cost model tracks only AI API spend (tokens and USD), but the real total cost of AI-assisted engineering also includes human time spent on: task formulation, prompt iteration, result review, partial-fit correction, and re-delegation.
  - This human oversight cost is currently invisible and may exceed the AI API cost on tasks with high retry pressure or partial-fit outcomes.
- Why it matters:
  - If true, optimizing for lower AI token cost without measuring human oversight cost may be misleading — a cheaper AI session that requires more human correction could be more expensive in total than a pricier session that produces exact fit on the first pass.
  - The P&L view cannot be trusted until both sides of cost are visible.
- Expected upside:
  - identify task types or failure modes where human correction cost dominates
  - reframe partial-fit as doubly expensive: AI cost was spent AND human rework time was spent
  - improve the cost-per-exact-fit metric by including the full cost, not just the API bill
- Expected resource cost:
  - medium — requires either manual time-logging or a proxy metric (e.g., review session duration, number of follow-up tasks spawned from a partial-fit closure)
- Main risks or where this may be wrong:
  - human time is hard to measure accurately without explicit tracking, which adds friction
  - the split between "AI session time" and "human review time" may be blurry in practice
  - on high-autonomy tasks the oversight cost may already be negligible, making this less urgent than it appears
- Alternatives considered:
  - ignore human time and treat AI cost as a sufficient proxy
  - use `attempts` as a rough proxy for oversight cost (each retry implies human involvement)
  - add explicit human-hours field to goal records and require manual entry
- Current confidence:
  - `low-medium`
- Evidence status:
  - no current data; entirely a structural gap identified through P&L framing
  - the `attempts_per_closed_task` metric (currently 1.04 overall, 1.14 for product) is the closest existing proxy, but it does not capture review time on successful first-pass tasks
- Next re-evaluation trigger:
  - after an experiment that manually logs human time on 5–10 tasks and compares it to AI cost
  - or after a proxy metric (e.g., follow-up task count per closed goal) is tried as an approximation
- Related hypotheses:
  - depends on H-027 (duration) as a prerequisite for any time-based cost model
  - relates to H-002 (retry pressure) — retry pressure is currently the closest proxy for human overhead
- Notes:
  - `2026-04-04`: added from P&L analysis that identified human oversight as the invisible half of the total cost equation.
