# Historical conversation analysis may be a better metrics input than live manual tracking

Single-hypothesis file.

### H-008 — Historical conversation analysis may be a better metrics input than live manual tracking

- Status: `active`
- Created: `2026-04-02`
- Statement:
  - The most reliable way to track product and workflow metrics may be to keep a minimal live capture for essential invariants and then reconstruct and analyze the full history of our conversation and task outcomes afterward, rather than trying to make the agent write fully structured metrics live during work.
- Why it matters:
  - If true, the metrics workflow should prioritize retrospective extraction from conversation history as the main analysis layer, while keeping live instrumentation small enough that it does not distract from the task itself.
- Expected upside:
  - reduce friction during normal agent work
  - recover more complete history than hand-entered updates usually capture
  - make metrics less dependent on the agent remembering to write them in the moment
  - preserve enough live structure to keep core invariants trustworthy
  - improve consistency by deriving metrics from what actually happened, not only from what was explicitly recorded live
- Main risks or where this may be wrong:
  - conversation history may be incomplete, noisy, or harder to parse than structured live entries
  - some important signals may never appear clearly enough in chat history to reconstruct reliably
  - a minimal live layer may still be necessary for timestamps, status transitions, and other invariant fields
  - retrospective extraction can lag behind the work and reduce immediacy for operational decisions
- Alternatives considered:
  - live manual metric entry during each task
  - a pure retrospective model with no live capture at all
  - stricter agent prompts that force structured updates in real time
- Current confidence:
  - `medium`
- Evidence status:
  - supported by the recurring risk that live metric capture becomes a separate chore instead of a natural part of the work
  - not yet validated against a full historical reconstruction pipeline or a minimal live-capture design
- Next re-evaluation trigger:
  - after we test whether historical chat extraction can recover goal, attempt, and outcome data while a small live layer still keeps invariants reliable
- Work plan:
  - Ingest: read `~/.codex` sources into a local raw warehouse without trying to interpret them yet.
  - Normalize: map threads, sessions, events, messages, logs, and usage into stable raw tables.
  - Derive: build reusable marts for goals, attempts, timelines, retry chains, and usage slices.
  - Validate: compare reconstructed records against current live metrics and check where the raw history is reliable or incomplete.
  - Decide: keep the minimal live layer only for invariants that the transcript cannot recover safely.
- Notes:
