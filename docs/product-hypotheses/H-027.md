# Task duration may be required before the revenue side of P&L can be estimated

Single-hypothesis file.

### H-027 — Task duration may be required before the revenue side of P&L can be estimated

- Status: `active`
- Created: `2026-04-04`
- Statement:
  - `started_at` and `finished_at` are already stored per goal, but no aggregate duration metric exists in the summary.
  - Without median or total task duration, it is impossible to estimate the revenue side of P&L — i.e., what the same work would have cost without AI assistance.
- Why it matters:
  - If true, the product currently tracks only the cost side of P&L while the "engineering profit" framing in `docs/product-framing.md` implies a revenue side exists but is never measured.
  - Knowing duration unlocks: estimated human-equivalent cost, ROI per task type, and trend analysis (are tasks getting faster over time?).
- Expected upside:
  - enable a first estimate of engineering profit: `human_equivalent_cost - ai_cost`
  - surface trend signals: is product delivery getting faster after process changes?
  - give context to retry pressure: two attempts in 20 minutes is different from two attempts over 3 hours
- Expected resource cost:
  - low — data is already captured; the work is aggregation and reporting only
- Main risks or where this may be wrong:
  - wall-clock duration is a noisy proxy for effort because sessions get interrupted
  - comparing AI-assisted duration to human-equivalent cost requires an assumed hourly rate that may not be agreed on
  - duration alone conflates fast-bad with fast-good the same way token speed does (see H-014)
- Alternatives considered:
  - skip duration and estimate ROI from task count alone
  - use token count as a proxy for effort instead of wall-clock duration
  - require manual effort estimates per task rather than deriving from timestamps
- Current confidence:
  - `medium`
- Evidence status:
  - no current aggregate; raw timestamps exist in every goal record but are not surfaced in `show` or `render-report`
  - back-of-envelope suggests ROI could be 10–30x on AI API cost alone, but human oversight time is unknown and may close the gap significantly
- Next re-evaluation trigger:
  - after duration aggregates are added to the summary
  - or after a before/after comparison on a real workflow change uses duration as part of the analysis
- Notes:
  - `2026-04-04`: added from P&L analysis that identified the revenue side of engineering profit as entirely absent from current metrics.
