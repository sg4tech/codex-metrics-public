# Growth and Pricing

How the product reaches users and captures value from them.

## Canonical sources — growth

- **Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown** — *Hacking Growth*. Codified the growth experiment process (ideate → prioritize → test → analyze).
- **Andrew Chen** — *The Cold Start Problem*. The best treatment of network effects and bootstrapping multi-sided markets. The "atomic network" framing.
- **Brian Balfour** (Reforge) — the "4 Fits" essay series: product-market fit, market-channel fit, channel-model fit, model-market fit. Explains why products that scale in one segment fail in another.
- **Casey Winters** (Reforge / ex-Pinterest, Grubhub) — growth loops vs funnels; SEO loops; marketplace growth.
- **Elena Verna** — PLG motion, growth loops, self-serve B2B.
- **Kyle Poyar** (OpenView) — PLG playbooks, benchmarks, the PLG-to-sales handoff.
- **Bob Moesta** — *Demand-Side Sales*. Reframing "selling" as "helping the customer make progress."

## Canonical sources — pricing

- **Madhavan Ramanujam** — *Monetizing Innovation*. Price before product. Willingness-to-pay interviews as core PM skill. Four price-setting mistakes (feature shocks, minivations, hidden gems, undead).
- **Hermann Simon** — *Confessions of the Pricing Man*. Strategic pricing from the founder of Simon-Kucher.
- **Patrick Campbell** (ProfitWell / Paddle) — the largest public SaaS pricing research corpus.

## Core concepts — growth

- **Growth loops, not funnels.** Loops compound: output becomes input. Funnels leak. A growth loop is characterized by a reinvested output (e.g., content → SEO rank → traffic → more content).
- **4 Fits (Balfour).** Product-market (customers love it), market-channel (channels that reach those customers), channel-model (monetization matches channel economics), model-market (revenue math works at the market's ACV). All four must align.
- **Channel-product fit.** Products are built around channels; changing channels usually requires rebuilding the product.
- **Atomic network (Chen).** The smallest stable network — below which the product has no value, above which it becomes self-sustaining.
- **PLG motion.** Self-serve product is the distribution; sales augments in enterprise tail. Works when time-to-value is short and value is demonstrable solo.
- **Hair-on-fire users.** The early-stage buyer has an acute, existing, painful problem. Find these; ignore mild interest.

## Core concepts — pricing

- **Willingness-to-pay (WTP) research.** Price is a property of the customer's perception, not the product's cost. Research with Van Westendorp questions or conjoint analysis before launch, not after.
- **Price metric.** The unit of the price (per seat, per event, per GB, per outcome). Choosing the right metric is often worth more than choosing the right number.
- **Value-based pricing.** Anchor on value delivered, not cost incurred or competition.
- **Four pricing failure modes (Ramanujam).** Feature shocks (too many tiers), minivations (under-priced), hidden gems (unshipped features customers would pay for), undead (products kept alive past their economic expiry).
- **Cost-plus pricing.** The default wrong answer. Ignores what customers value and what competitors do.

## Practical principles

- **Growth is a consequence of product + distribution + monetization alignment, not a standalone function.** Bolting a growth team onto a misaligned product does not fix it.
- **Channels change faster than products.** Diversify; don't build a single-channel dependency.
- **Run WTP research before you set a number.** Ten customer conversations about price will calibrate you better than a spreadsheet.
- **Don't compete on price unless you have a structural cost advantage.** Margin erosion is permanent; differentiation is recoverable.
- **Annual contracts improve retention math, not retention reality.** They defer churn measurement; they do not prevent churn.
