# Craft: Team, Career, Writing, Operating Cadence

The PM's personal discipline: how you work, communicate, and grow.

## Canonical sources — team and career

- **Marty Cagan** (SVPG) — *Inspired*, *Empowered*, *Transformed*. Product trio (PM + design + engineering). Empowered product teams. The difference between product teams and feature teams.
- **Will Larson** — *An Elegant Puzzle*, *Staff Engineer*. Written for engineering leadership but highly PM-readable; systems for running a team.
- **Camille Fournier** — *The Manager's Path*. Individual contributor → tech lead → manager → director transitions.
- **Julie Zhuo** — *The Making of a Manager*. The first-time-manager playbook; clearest voice on running 1:1s and giving feedback.
- **Ravi Mehta** — Product Competency Toolkit (ravi-mehta.com). Free. The most concrete career framework for PMs (four core competencies × four seniority levels).
- **Bill Campbell** (via *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt/Rosenberg/Eagle) — coaching culture; the "make everyone around you better" operating standard.
- **Michael Lopp (Rands)** — *Managing Humans*. Engineering-management essays, PM-adjacent.
- **Lenny Rachitsky** — Lenny's Newsletter / Podcast. Aggregator; vast case-study library. Treat as index, not primary source.

## Canonical sources — writing

- **Amazon** — the PR-FAQ and 6-pager culture (silent reading at meeting start).
- **Paul Graham** — essays (paulgraham.com). Clarity-of-thought exemplars, orthogonal to PM but formative.
- **Julian Shapiro** — writing guides (julian.com/guide/write). Practical non-fiction writing.
- **Stripe Press** — their book catalog sets the aesthetic and quality bar for business writing.

## Canonical sources — operating

- **Shreyas Doshi** — LNO framework (Leverage / Neutral / Overhead tasks); product sense essays; "high-agency."
- **Andy Grove** — *High Output Management*. Still the best operations management book. OKRs originated here (as MBOs adapted).
- **David Allen** — *Getting Things Done*. Personal task flow; the "inbox" discipline.

## Core frameworks

- **Product trio.** A product team = PM + design + tech lead, working as peers on outcomes. Not PM "managing" design and eng.
- **Empowered vs feature teams (Cagan).** An empowered team is given problems and outcomes to solve. A feature team is given a roadmap to execute. The difference compounds in decision quality over years.
- **LNO (Doshi).** Classify tasks before doing them: **Leverage** (10× outcome payoff, do these deeply), **Neutral** (proportional payoff, do adequately), **Overhead** (small/linear payoff, minimize).
- **High-agency.** The disposition to find a way when the stated way is blocked. Contrast with the low-agency posture that waits for process.
- **PR-FAQ.** Write the press release + FAQ before building. Forces customer-facing clarity.
- **6-pager.** Narrative memo, read silently first 20 minutes of a meeting. Replaces slide decks that obscure weak thinking.

## Practical principles

- **Writing > talking.** Written arguments hold up to scrutiny that spoken ones slip past. Decisions you can't write down are decisions you haven't made.
- **One number you own.** A PM without a primary metric is an assistant project manager.
- **Operating cadence: weekly / monthly / quarterly.** Weekly review (what moved, what didn't, what next). Monthly strategy check-in (is the plan still right?). Quarterly replanning (update the plan).
- **Document decisions (ADR / decisions log).** The next PM — or you in three months — will need to know *why*, not just what.
- **Craft in one underlying discipline.** Great PMs have depth in one of: data, design, engineering, research, sales. "Pure" PMs without craft are dependent on others for every load-bearing judgment.
- **Make everyone around you better.** Campbell standard. If you are the bottleneck for people's growth, you are a cost center.
